Business Formation & Structure

Complete guide to setting up your business legally and strategically

Choose Your Business Entity

🏢 Sole Proprietorship

Best for: Freelancers, consultants, side hustles

  • Pros: Easy setup, low cost, full control
  • Cons: Personal liability, harder to raise capital
  • Tax: Report on personal Schedule C

🏛️ LLC (Limited Liability Company)

Best for: Small to medium businesses, real estate, e-commerce

  • Pros: Personal asset protection, flexible taxation, simpler than corporation
  • Cons: State-specific rules, annual fees
  • Tax: Pass-through (or elect S-Corp status)
  • Where to form: Your State Secretary of State

🏢 S-Corporation

Best for: Businesses with $50K+ profit, want to save on self-employment tax

  • Pros: Tax savings, employee benefits, credibility
  • Cons: More paperwork, payroll requirements, IRS scrutiny
  • Tax: Pass-through with salary + distributions
  • Requirements: Must be US-based, max 100 shareholders

🏦 C-Corporation

Best for: High-growth startups, seeking investors, planning IPO

  • Pros: Unlimited shareholders, easier fundraising, perpetual existence
  • Cons: Double taxation, complex compliance, expensive
  • Tax: Corporate tax + shareholder dividends taxed

Trust Structures for Asset Protection

Why Use a Trust?

  • Protect personal assets from business liabilities
  • Keep ownership private (trusts don't appear in public records)
  • Hold multiple businesses under one umbrella
  • Estate planning and wealth transfer
  • Tax advantages when structured properly

🔐 Revocable Living Trust

Purpose: Hold personal and business assets, maintain control

  • You remain trustee (full control)
  • Avoid probate when you pass
  • Can be changed anytime
  • Note: Does NOT protect from creditors while alive

🛡️ Irrevocable Trust

Purpose: Maximum asset protection, estate tax savings

  • Assets legally separated from you (creditor protection)
  • Cannot be changed easily
  • Reduces estate tax liability
  • Use case: Hold LLCs, real estate, intellectual property

🏢 Business Trust (a.k.a. Massachusetts Trust)

Purpose: Operate multiple businesses under one structure

  • Trust holds all your business entities
  • Privacy: beneficial owners not public
  • Can own LLCs, S-Corps, C-Corps, real estate
  • Example: Trust owns 3 LLCs (e-commerce, consulting, real estate)

⚖️ Legal Disclaimer

This is educational information only. Consult a licensed attorney and CPA before setting up trust structures. Laws vary by state. For attorney referrals: Avvo.com or LegalZoom.com

Banking & Credit Strategies (Historical & Modern)

📜 Legacy Wisdom: The 3-Bank Method

A financial rotation strategy used during the 1930s–1950s, particularly in underserved communities. This was NOT illegal—it was wisdom-based survival, rooted in understanding bank timing and trust.

🏦 What Was the 3-Bank Method?

Historical Context: Before credit scores existed, your reputation and deposit behavior determined creditworthiness.

The System (How It Worked):
  1. Borrow from Bank A - Use your character and name to secure a small loan
  2. Deposit into Bank B - Build deposit history and perceived capital
  3. Make payments to Bank A - Create good repayment record
  4. Borrow from Bank B - Bank B sees consistency, extends credit
  5. Deposit into Bank C - Repeat the pattern
  6. Continue servicing all loans - Using revenue and timing
  7. Eventually withdraw from Bank C - Pay off Bank A, complete cycle
Why It Worked Then:
  • Float time: Banks processed transactions slowly (days/weeks)
  • Relationships: You were known and trusted at your local bank
  • Manual processing: Paper trails moved slowly
  • Integrity mattered: This was leverage built on character and repayment

⚠️ Modern Reality Check

This strategy doesn't work the same way today because:

  • Electronic banking processes transactions instantly
  • Credit bureaus track all your accounts nationwide
  • Banks share data through centralized systems
  • Attempting "bank kiting" is now illegal (even unintentionally)

However, the PRINCIPLES still apply:

  • Build deposit relationships at multiple banks
  • Establish payment history and trust
  • Understand timing (but don't exploit it illegally)
  • Use reputation and consistency as leverage

🏛️ Other Historical Informal Finance Systems

ROSCA (Rotating Savings & Credit Associations):

  • Susu/Sou-sou (West Africa, Caribbean)
  • Stokvel (South Africa)
  • Tanda (Latin America)
  • Hui (China)
  • Chit Funds (India - Patel/Gujarati communities)
  • Gemach (Jewish interest-free loan funds)

These community-based systems relied on:

  • Relational trust (not credit scores)
  • Mutual accountability
  • Collective gain

Debt Elimination Calculator

Debt Snowball Method

Pay off smallest debts first for quick wins and motivation

  1. List debts from smallest to largest balance
  2. Make minimum payments on all debts
  3. Put extra money toward smallest debt
  4. When paid off, roll that payment to next smallest
  5. Repeat until debt-free

Debt Avalanche Method

Pay off highest interest rate debts first to save money

  1. List debts from highest to lowest interest rate
  2. Make minimum payments on all debts
  3. Put extra money toward highest rate
  4. When paid off, roll payment to next highest rate
  5. Saves more money in interest
